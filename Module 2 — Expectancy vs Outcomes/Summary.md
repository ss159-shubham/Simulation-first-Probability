### The Central Lesson
**Positive expectancy does not guarantee positive experience.**

Expectancy exists in the population.  
Experience exists along a single path.  

Confusing the two is the root cause of most emotional interference, premature abandonment, and system-hopping behavior.

---
### Key Insights Synthesized

1. Winning systems feel bad most of the time.     
  A large majority of the time is spent below equity highs, in drawdowns or stagnation.

2. Variance dominates before expectancy asserts itself.  
  Early and intermediate outcomes are unreliable signals of system quality.

3. Losing periods are structural, not diagnostic.  
  They are expected features of probabilistic systems, not evidence of failure.

4. Identical rules do not produce identical lives.  
  Outcome dispersion across agents and across time is unavoidable.

5. Short samples are emotionally loud and statistically weak.  
  Confidence and despair peak precisely when information quality is lowest.

6. Pain is continuous; reward is discrete.     
  The psychological cost is paid trade by trade, while expectancy pays only occasionally.

---
### Reframed Mental Model
- Drawdowns are not mistakes
- Discomfort is not a warning signal
- Smoothness is not a property of edges

> Drawdowns are the operating expense of expectancy.

### Why This Module Matters?

Most people do not fail because they lack an edge.     
They fail because they are unprepared for what an edge actually feels like.     

Module 2 recalibrates expectations so that emotional reactions are no longer mistaken for information.
